TrymenT breakdown and impressions

1. YoU KnowS ThE DeaR KnowS

First is the title itself.

To someone with a basic knowledge of English, at first glance “YoU Know(S)” might seem like a typo, but knowing the writer, this was done on purpose.

The real question is the meaning behind the sentence. Is it simply “You knows the dear knows”, or “You knows; the dear knows”?

In my opinion, the use of the word “The” itself seems unnatural, why not use “that”? I could simply be looking too much into it.

2. Kakeru’s camera – Alpha

In the beginning of the game, Kakeru’s holding a camera with the word “Alpha” written on it. I believe it to be a hand-me-down from Alpha. In the end, the camera was passed down to Ayame. It could simply be the brand of the camera.

3. Kakeru’s hairpin

Interestingly enough, Kakeru’s wearing a hairpin that’s shaped like Delta (Δ), the fourth letter of the Greek alphabet. Fumi’s wearing two “II” hairpins. Mhlia’s wearing two “X” hairpins, which could either mean 10 in Roman numerals or the 22nd Greek alphabet, chi (Χ), while she didn’t wear them during childhood. This could have some deeper meaning behind it, or merely be an aesthetic choice.

4. “Clock” what’s the breakdown

If you pay attention to the dates, you’ll notice the Roman numerals don't tell the time, but rather the month. What does the Greek alphabet mean, then?

At first I thought they were used to show the perspective, with Ψ being Ayame’s group’s perspective and Ω being Tsukasa’s.

However, starting from Op.3, there are more variations to the alphabet.
If we line all the years all up ascendingly, we can see a pattern.

2032 Τ
2033 Υ
2034 Φ
2035 Χ
2036 Ψ
2037 Ω

Each alphabet corresponds to a year according to their order.
That means this isn’t a clock that tells the time, but rather the date.
This revelation is important for my future theories.

5. Map, name symbolism

The map contains a lot of references to the prequel “Traumend” games in the form of names. As I have not played any of the prequels, I cannot understand their significance.

6. Apple Juice and Mhlia

In one of the scenes still within the confines of the trial version, Tsukasa asked Mhlia to pick out a drink from the vending machine. She chose the apple juice.

I could be wrong here, but this seems to be symbolism.
Mhlia Blanco’s name is interesting, as we find out later that Mikuni named her that for her name as an observation AI.

If I’m not wrong, Mhlia means Apple tree (μηλιά) in Greek, while Blanco means white in Spanish.

One of the fruits most commonly associated with the forbidden fruit is the apple.
The chapter where Mhlia introduced herself is Op. 2 Z禁断の果実 (forbidden fruit).
The tree of the forbidden fruit is also known as the tree of the knowledge of good and evil. The fruit that gave the first man and woman the awareness of good and evil parallels how Mhlia divulged the knowledge that the world isn’t real to Tsukasa (and the readers).

I’m not sure what Blanco could mean. If I had to take a stab at it, I would say it means Mhlia’s intentions are pure. While the serpent who tempted Eve’s intentions are “black”, Mhlia’s are “white”. It could also just be that Mikuni’s naming sense is awful and he named her after her hair color.

7. May 20, Sunday

After Tsukasa woke up, he asked one of the joggers for the date. She said May 20, Sunday. A search would tell you that the years after 2000 and before 2050 that have the day Sunday on May 20 are 2018, 2029, 2035, 2040, 2046.

Of course, later you’d find out that none of these are true.
If you have optimism, you’d think that the world in TrymenT is different from our world, if not, you’d think that the writers didn’t check the calendar.

9. Mhlia • B • Horatria (not official translation)

Now that we know what Mhlia Blanco means, what does Horatria mean?

ホラhora (χώρα) = 国(country), トリアtria(τρία) = 三 (three)
If we combined them into one word, it’ll mean 三国, her surname.

51. Op. 3 :||:

When the final chapter came, I was surprised to learn that the chapter’s alphabet was :||: instead of N.

Searching everywhere, I could not find the meaning to the symbol until I re-watched the OP and ED.

Could it be that :||: = 𝄇𝄆 ?

If so, the M𝄇𝄑𝄆N in the movie can be rearranged to become 𝄆N~ 𝄌~M𝄇

If you don’t know, in music, a repeat sign indicates that a section should be repeated. When you reach 𝄇, you either jump back to the beginning, or to 𝄆 if the piece has one.

Man, the unwilting sakura trees, the bananas, and the D.C symbol above really reminds me of another galge.

With that knowledge in mind, I have several theories.

(1) M = Mhlia, N = Nitta

(2) M = 2037, N = 2026

The first theory is that chapter M ends with Mhlia being the lead, while chapter N, presumably the first chapter in -Omega-, starts with Nitta.

The second theory uses a previous theory, where the alphabet corresponds with years. If we assume the year of Ω = the year of chapter M = 2037, and start counting back from Ω to N, we’ll get the year 2026.

Why 2026?

Mikuni-sensei said in 2037 that the Technological singularity happened 10 years prior, 2027. Therefore in the -Omega chapter- sequel, we may be taking a look into how the Technological Singularity happened.

𝄑 and 𝄌 could just be placeholders, or they could mean what they are supposed to be in music.

𝄑 is fermata, a sign which means pause for an unspecified length. My theory is that it has something to do with the protected server the three marked people are in.

𝄌 is the coda symbol, I think. I’m not familiar with that variation of coda.

Other things I found that would back up the theory is in the movies. Starting at 0:38 of the TrymenT teaser movie, Greek letters appear, transitioning from the last to first;

while in the TrymenT OP movie, the frames go back in reverse chronological order starting at 1:48,

finally stopping at the place Tsukasa saw in his flashback, where 4 people were talking about how the tree was beautiful “like sakura”. I assume the flashback’s the “start” we’ll go back to when we “re:start”

This theory is absurd, and is highly unlikely to be true. Personally I believe that :||: being a question isn’t wrong, and the direction I went in isn’t wrong as well, but I made too many leaps in logic and committed logical fallacy.

The 𝄇𝄆 symbol itself could mean “Re:”, as in “Re:LieF”, “Re:TrymenT”.

I always wondered why “Re:LieF” has a “:” in it.

I’ve always thought Re:LieF sounded like Relive, which can mean再体験する, and re:live would mean人生を再スタート/新生活. Relief itself can mean安心or救助

However, recently I found that in English, lief can mean dear. If that’s so, “Re:LieF” can be written as “Re: Dear”. It would make sense, since one of the goals of TrymenT is to save Nitta, whom I assume is Dear.

That could also mean that the “Re:LieF” sign in the opening sequence isn’t the name of the building, but an event.

Re:LieF.
The return of Dear.

That’s my theory.
